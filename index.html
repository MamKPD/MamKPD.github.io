Abstract

2D keypoint detection that aims to locate every joint for the human or animal is important in computer vision tasks. Despite conventional CNN-based and Transformer-based methods achieving impressive results, these approaches suffer from balancing efficiency and performance. This paper aims to propose a novel, simple, yet effective baseline for addressing 2D kepoint detection tasks. The state space model (SSM) has recently received much attention due to its powerful performance and efficiency. This paper proposes a novel, simple Mamba-based framework for 2D Keypoint Detection named MamKPD, which firstly applies the state space model to detect keypoints for humans and animals. Specifically, we propose to use the Mamba block following patch merging as the encoder. Patch merging aggregates information from different patches, which enhances the multi-scale feature extraction capability of the Mamba block. In addition, we adopt a heatmap decoder consisting of several transposed convolutional layers to obtain positional information on joints. We conduct extensive experiments on commonly used benchmarks, including the MS COCO, MPII, and AP-10K datasets, where the proposed PoseMamba achieves competitive results with minimal parameters.
